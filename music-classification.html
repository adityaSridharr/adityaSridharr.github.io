Fusion of Machine Learning and Musical Expertise

My publication, Attention-Guided Spectrogram Sequence Modeling with CNNs for Music Genre Classification, represents the intersection of my two greatest passions: technology and music. As a professional Indian classical musician, I developed an attention-based classifier that achieved state-of-the-art accuracy in music genre classification. This project marked my first deep dive into machine learning and provided fascinating insights into genre-specific patterns and temporal features in music.

Key Achievements
  • Published research paper on arxiv.org showcasing novel attention mechanisms for music analysis
  • Achieved state-of-the-art accuracy in genre classification using semi-supervised learning
  • Successfully integrated deep embeddings to overcome data limitations
  • Created interpretable visualizations of how the model "hears" different genres
  • Developed methods to identify which temporal parts of music are most indicative of genre
Technologies Used
  • Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) for spectrogram analysis
  • Attention mechanisms to highlight genre-specific features
  • Semi-supervised learning techniques to overcome limited labeled data
  • Deep neural embeddings for advanced feature extraction
  • Spectrogram sequence modeling for temporal music analysis
  • Visualization techniques for model interpretability
Challenges Overcome
  • Creating an explainable model that avoids "black-box" decision making
  • Developing techniques to understand which parts of music influence classification
  • Working with limited labeled music data while maintaining accuracy
  • Balancing computational efficiency with model performance
  • Translating musical knowledge into machine learning architecture design

Research Approach

Motivation & Problem Framing

Inspired by my background as a professional Indian classical musician, I sought to understand if machines could recognize patterns in music the way humans do, moving beyond treating genre classification as a simple classification problem.

Innovation in Methodology

Introduced attention mechanisms to pinpoint which parts of a spectrogram were most important for genre classification, allowing the model to focus on the most distinctive temporal and frequency features.

Addressing Explainability

Tackled the challenge of explainability by developing techniques to visualize and interpret the model's decisions, making it possible to understand why certain segments of music were classified into specific genres.

Data Limitation Solutions

Implemented semi-supervised learning and deep embeddings to overcome the challenge of limited labeled music data, efficiently leveraging both labeled and unlabeled examples.

Publication & Impact

Published findings on arxiv.org, contributing new insights to the field of music information retrieval and demonstrating the potential of combining machine learning with domain expertise in music.
