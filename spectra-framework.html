Unveiling the Inner Workings of Black-Box Models

SPECTRA (Semantic Perturbation-Based Counterfactuals and Training for Robustness against Adversarial Attacks) is an innovative framework I designed for my Explainability in ML course to uncover the inner workings of black-box models. Driven by my curiosity about how image classifiers "understand" images, this framework generates semantically faithful counterfactuals by perturbing latent space activations. The approach significantly improves model interpretability while increasing prediction accuracy by over 15% and introducing novel metrics for measuring adversarial vulnerability.

Research Methodology

Problem Identification

Recognized the critical gap in understanding how black-box models, particularly image classifiers, make decisions and the need for greater transparency in AI systems.

Framework Development

Designed the SPECTRA framework to generate semantically faithful counterfactuals by systematically perturbing latent space activations, providing insights into model decision boundaries.

Stability Enhancement

Introduced a novel stability regularization term specifically engineered to bolster classifier resilience against adversarial attacks while maintaining performance on legitimate inputs.

Metric Creation

Developed the 'SPECTRA Attackability' metric to provide a standardized way of quantifying a model's vulnerability to adversarial examples, filling a significant gap in model evaluation techniques.

Validation & Results

Demonstrated significant improvements in both model interpretability and performance, achieving over 15% increase in prediction accuracy while simultaneously enhancing model robustness.
